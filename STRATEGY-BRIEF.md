### Universal Patterns (stolen from the top 10)

1. **Slide 1 = hook only.** Big question or claim. NO logo-first designs. Hook > brand.
2. **One idea per slide.** Never cram two concepts.
3. **Massive type.** If it's readable on a watch, it works on a phone.
4. **Slide count = 8-10.** More = drop-off, fewer = not "document-worthy".
5. **Slide 9/10 = recap.** Summarize all points on one slide.
6. **Final slide = soft CTA.** Not "buy now". It's "save", "share", "follow for more".
7. **Slide numbers.** `01/10` bottom-corner = subliminal "finish it" signal.
8. **Brand bar (quiet, not loud).** Bottom strip, never hijacks the hook.
9. **Contrast + hierarchy.** Big → medium → small. No walls of text.
10. **Pattern interrupts.** Slide 3 or 5 breaks the rhythm (data viz, quote, shock stat).

## 2. Content Philosophy — "Not Always Selling"

**Target mix (out of 30):**
- 24 PDFs = pure value (frameworks, research, playbooks, tools, data)
- 4 PDFs = teachable case studies (lessons, not pitches)
- 2 PDFs = soft brand stories (founder POV)
- **0 hard sells.**

The user asked for "something amazing" — that means the PDF should make people DM you, not because you asked, but because they want more of the brain behind it.

## 4. The Design System — 6 Families, One DNA

**Problem with 30 completely different designs:** zero brand recognition.
**Problem with 30 identical designs:** boring, stops performing.
**Solution:** 6 visual "worlds" that all share SkynetLabs DNA.

### Shared DNA (every slide has these)
- **Format:** 1080×1350 (4:5) — works on LinkedIn, IG, FB, Pinterest
- **Brand bar** at bottom (72px): `SKYNET LABS  ·  @skynetjoe  ·  skynetjoe.com`
- **Slide counter** bottom-right: `03/10`
- **One idea per slide**
- **Mobile-safe margins:** 80px left/right, 120px top/bottom

### Family 1 — NEON TERMINAL (Category A: AI Tools)
**Vibe:** Retro CRT hacker terminal. Green phosphor. Scan lines.
- Background: Pure black `(0,0,0)` with subtle scan line overlay
- Primary: `NEON_GREEN (0,255,65)`
- Secondary: `AMBER (255,176,0)` for warnings/highlights
- Typography: **Consolas Bold** (monospace) for headers, Arial for body
- Accents: ASCII box borders, terminal prompt `> `, cursor blocks `█`
- Use case: Feels tool-review/hacker-authority

### Family 2 — CIRCUIT BOARD (Category B: Automation)
**Vibe:** PCB tech diagram. Electric blue. Trace lines.
- Background: Deep navy `(6,10,25)` with faint circuit trace pattern
- Primary: `ELECTRIC_BLUE (0,132,255)`
- Secondary: `NEON_CYAN (0,224,208)` for nodes
- Typography: Segoe UI Bold for headers, Arial for body
- Accents: Node dots, connection lines between concepts, flow arrows
- Use case: Visualizes workflow/process content naturally

### Family 3 — EXECUTIVE NAVY (Category C: Frameworks)
**Vibe:** McKinsey/HBR premium. Navy + gold. Authority.
- Background: Deep navy `(10,20,45)` with subtle gold grain
- Primary: `GOLD (212,175,55)`
- Secondary: `IVORY (245,240,225)` for body text
- Typography: **Georgia Bold** (serif) for headers, Segoe UI for body
- Accents: Gold hairlines, ornamental slide numbers, subtle quote marks
- Use case: Framework/playbook content that needs to feel "premium"

### Family 4 — DATA LAB (Category D: Research)
**Vibe:** Bloomberg terminal meets scientific journal.
- Background: Dark purple `(18,10,35)` gradient to black
- Primary: `HOT_PINK (255,56,100)` for key stats
- Secondary: `LIME (0,230,118)` for positive data, `AMBER` for warnings
- Typography: Arial Black for stats, Consolas for data labels
- Accents: Bar charts, horizontal progress bars, data tables, gridlines
- Use case: Research/data-heavy content with real numbers

### Family 5 — CASE FILE (Category E: Case Studies)
**Vibe:** Vintage investigator case file. Kraft paper. Red stamps.
- Background: Kraft/paper texture `(232,215,175)` with noise
- Primary: `INK_BLACK (25,20,15)` for body
- Secondary: `STAMP_RED (180,35,30)` for stamps/callouts
- Typography: **Courier New** (typewriter) for headers, Georgia for body
- Accents: "CASE #" stamp, red underlines, paperclip icons, tape strips
- Use case: Case studies feel authentic, like you're reading a file

### Family 6 — MINDSCAPE (Category F: Mental Models)
**Vibe:** Warm sunrise gradient. Organic. Thoughtful.
- Background: Gradient `ORANGE(255,140,0) → DEEP_PURPLE(88,30,120)`
- Primary: `CREAM (250,240,220)` for body
- Secondary: `GOLD` highlights, `SOFT_PINK (255,180,180)` accents
- Typography: Georgia for headers, Segoe UI Light for body
- Accents: Organic circles, soft glows, hand-drawn underlines
- Use case: Philosophical/mental-model content feels human, not corporate

## 5. Slide Architecture (10-slide template)

| # | Role | Content |
|---|------|---------|
| 01 | **HOOK** | Big claim or question. Huge type. No logo-first. |
| 02 | **PROBLEM** | Why this matters / what most people get wrong |
| 03 | **POINT 1** | First insight/tool/framework step |
| 04 | **POINT 2** | Second |
| 05 | **POINT 3** | Third (pattern interrupt — quote or stat) |
| 06 | **POINT 4** | Fourth |
| 07 | **POINT 5** | Fifth |
| 08 | **POINT 6** | Sixth (or key takeaway) |
| 09 | **RECAP** | All points summarized on one screen |
| 10 | **ENGAGE CTA** | Save + share + follow. No "buy". |

## 6. Interactive First Comment Strategies

**Rule:** First comment ≠ sales pitch. First comment = engagement trigger.

Rotating across 6 styles:

1. **Poll style:** "Which of these 10 are you using? Drop the number 👇"
2. **Challenge:** "Pick ONE tool from slide 5 and use it this week. Reply 'DONE' when you do."
3. **Opinion hook:** "Controversial take: slide 7 is wrong. Prove me otherwise in the replies."
4. **Fill-the-blank:** "My #1 AI workflow is ______. What's yours?"
5. **Swap/trade:** "I'll trade my full n8n template for YOUR best prompt. Drop it below."
6. **Resource drop:** "I turned all 10 of these into a free Notion template. Reply 'TEMPLATE' and I'll send it."

Each carousel rotates through these — never the same first comment twice in 30 posts.

## 7. GHL CSV Row Strategy

Every row publishes simultaneously to:
- **Facebook** (`type FB = post`) — native multi-image carousel
- **Instagram** (`type IG = post`) — native swipe carousel
- **LinkedIn** (`postAsPdf = TRUE`) — converted to document post
- **Pinterest** (each slide becomes a pin)
- **YouTube** (skipped — carousels don't apply)
- **TikTok** (skipped — carousels don't apply)
- **GBP** (skipped — no offer data)

Schedule: 1 post every 1-2 days over 6 weeks (Apr 10 → May 22, 2026).
